Historical Places in India

The Taj Mahal, an iconic white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India, is widely regarded as one of the most beautiful buildings in the world and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Commissioned by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, construction began in 1632 and was completed in 1653.

Agra Fort, located in Agra, India, is a majestic red sandstone fortress with a rich history that dates back to the Mughal era. Constructed by Emperor Akbar between 1565 and 1573, it served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors until Shah Jahan moved his capital to Delhi.

The Qutub Minar, located in Delhi, India, is a towering monument and a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its architectural splendor. Constructed in 1193 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, the founder of the Delhi Sultanate, it stands at 73 meters (240 feet) tall, making it the tallest brick minaret in the world.

The Golden Temple, or Sri Harmandir Sahib, is a renowned spiritual and cultural hub for Sikhs located in Amritsar, Punjab, India. The temple's architecture blends Islamic, Hindu, and European styles, featuring a striking gold-covered central shrine and a surrounding Amrit Sarovar, or holy tank, known for its supposed healing properties.
